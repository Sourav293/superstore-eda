# Superstore Sales — Exploratory Data Analysis

## Overview
End-to-end exploratory data analysis on a US retail 
superstore dataset containing 9994 orders from 2015-2018.
The goal was to uncover business insights and provide 
actionable recommendations.

## Dataset
- Source: Kaggle — Superstore Sales Dataset
- Rows: 9994 orders
- Columns: 21 features
- Period: 2015 — 2018

## Tools & Libraries
- Python 3.11
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

## Business Questions Answered
1. Which region generates most revenue and profit?
2. Which product category is most profitable?
3. How does discount affect profit?
4. Which customer segment brings most value?
5. What is the monthly sales trend?
6. Who are the top 10 customers by sales?
7. Which states have highest and lowest profit?
8. Which shipping mode is most used and profitable?

## Key Findings
- West region leads in both sales and profit
- Technology is the most profitable category
- Tables and Bookcases are loss-making products
- Discounts above 20% consistently cause losses
- Texas and Ohio losses caused by 32-37% avg discounts
- Consumer segment generates highest revenue
- December is peak sales and profit month
- Standard Class is most used shipping mode

## Business Recommendations
1. Cap discounts at 20% across all regions
2. Immediately review discount policy in Texas and Ohio
3. Reprice or discontinue Tables product line
4. Invest in Q4 marketing to maximize peak season
5. Create loyalty program for top 10 customers
